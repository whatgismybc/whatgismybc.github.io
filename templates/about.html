Pill-specific information is hard to find on the internet.

I chose the first birth control pill my doctor recommended. Later, I read This is Your Brain on Birth Control , by Sarah E. Hill. In it was a helpful table I wish I'd seen earlier, mapping hormone types to common side effects. After some Googling (too much), I found out that my pill was a second generation contraceptive and that I might benefit from looking into different pills.

Data

Generation-specific side effects come from the book This is Your Brain on Birth Control and this article .

Mappings from contraceptive methods to progestins, generic names, and alternate names were collected from drugs.com. Python dictionaries that you can play with (mappings for pill names to progestins, generic names, and alternate names) can be found here. The dataset covers 323 birth control pills.

Limitations

This website only covers oral contraceptives. While this isn't a replacement for talking to your doctor about contraceptive options (of which the pill is one of many), it can be a place to get started. Navigating the different progestin types can be useful to finding a pill that works! On top of that, the list is likely not comprehensive - if your pill isn't covered, email me at divyas@mit.edu!
